<h1>A Life Lived on the Montana Frontier</h1>
<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">In 1863, fire adventurers camping in a gulch high in the Rocky Mountains. <font id=1>happened upon</font> one of the largest gold deposits in North America. Within a year, 10,000 people had rushed to Alder Gulch, the Montana Territory was established, and nearby Virginia City became <font id=2>it's</font> first incorporated town and territorial capital. <font id=3>3</font></div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">In 1871, fifteen-year-old Sarah Gammon-an African American born in North Carolina and then raised by her aunt in Tennessee after the Civil War-was hired by Judge Murphytohelp care for his three children on the long trek by covered wagon to the Montana Territory. Judge Murphy had been called to serve <font id=4>as</font> the magistrate of Virginia City.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Once settled in Virginia City and accustomed to life in this gold-rush boomtown, Sarah found work as a hotel chambermaid. In 1873, she married William Brown, but her husband passed away <font id=5>less than</font> a decade later.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">In 1884, Sarah remarried, this time to a successful <font id=6>man who was twenty years older than she and who was also a gold miner, </font> Stephen Bickford. The Bickfords were an enterprising <font id=7>couple. Purchasing</font> two-thirds of the Virginia City Water Company in 1888, <font id=8>On</font> their stewardship, the city water service improved. They replaced the hollowed-out logs used to transport water with metal <font id=9>pipes, they</font> allowed for the introduction of indoor plumbing.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;"><font id=12> [1] <font id=10>Keeping change from the sale of farm products in a sugar bowl, </font> Sarah also ran the family <font id=11>farm</font> selling fruit, vegetables, poultry, and dairy products to people in the community. [2] After her husband's death in 1900, she continued to oversee, both operations. [3] She took a correspondence course in business management and then acquired the remaining one-third of the water company, becoming the sole proprietor. [4] With help from two of her children, Elmer and Virginia, Sarah managed the company until her death in 1931. </font></div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Sidestepped by progress and <font id=13>frozen</font> in time, Virginia City is now a living historical <font id=14>museum, preserving, </font> a slice of frontier life. As one of the state's earliest settlers and businesspeople, Sarah Gammon Bickford holds an important place in its history.</div>
