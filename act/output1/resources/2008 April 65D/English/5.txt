
<h1>"All I Can Do Is Take a Picture"</h1>
<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">[1]</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Ernest C. Withers has been recording history with his camera for more than sixty years. For most of his life, Withers has lived and worked as a photojournalist in Memphis, Tennessee, where he covered newsworthy events, both local and national, <font id=61>over some six decades.</font></div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">As an African American intimately familiar with the geography and people of the South, he was often the first photographer present as historic moments took place. Using his hometown as his <font id=62>base and documenting</font> the key people and events of <font id=63>the world in which he grew up, observed, and learned.</font></div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">[2]</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;"><font id=67>[1] <font id=64>When</font> his older sister's boyfriend showed no interest in using a camera that she had bought for him. Withers took it to school and photographed his classmates. [2] Years later, while serving as a jeep driver in World War II, he received permission from his company commander to train at the photography school at Camp Sutton, North Carolina. [3] Withers started taking pictures <font id=65>in</font> his youth. [4] In 1946, he left, the Army and began working <font id=66>at a job that was</font> a self-employed photographer.</font></div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">[3]</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Withers's profession gave him access to famous people. He has photographed seven of the last eight U.S. presidents and every major civil rights leader since the 1950s. <font id=68>Thus, he is well</font> known and well liked, Withers often traveled with and photographed <font id=69>such historic figures as</font> Martin Luther King Jr., Medgar Evers, and James Meredith. <font id=70>For instance, his</font> photos of Memphis's Beale Street jazz and blues musicians <font id=71>includes</font> the likes of B. B. King, Aretha Franklin, and Elvis Presley.</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">[4]</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">In addition to capturing many public personages on film, Withers also <font id=72>photographed: waitresses,</font> church congregations, nightclub audiences, and Little League baseball games. "I can't play a piano, I can't play a guitar, all I can do is take a picture," Withers said in a recent interview. <font id=73>At long last,</font> Ernest C. Withers had recorded some five million photographic images.</div>
