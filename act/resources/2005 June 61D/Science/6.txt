<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">The concentration of dissolved oxygen (DO) in river water affects the fish in the river. Many freshwater fish species, including crappie, perch, and bass, require a minimum average DO concentration of 5 parts per million (ppm) to thrive. Other fish, such as carp and gar, can tolerate average DO concentrations as low as 3 ppm. Table 1 shows the DO concentration at saturation for water at various temperatures. Figures 1 and 2 show the measured changes in a river’s water temperature and DO concentration, respectively, over a 24-hour period in midsummer. Table 2 shows how the DO concentration affects fish species in a river.</div>
