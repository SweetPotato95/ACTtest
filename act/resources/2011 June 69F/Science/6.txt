<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">A teacher described the procedure of a study to students in a science class:</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">A 1 kg sphere, Sphere X, and a 2 kg sphere, Sphere Y, were released from rest, one at a time, from Point P on the right side of a frictionless, U-shaped incline. Hp was the height of Point P above Point L, the lowest point on the incline (see Figure 1).</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;"><img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\196.png' width = '259'height = '186'/></div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Each sphere was allowed to slide as far up the left side of the mcline as it could go.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Next, the teacher gave the students the following definitions:</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">1. GPEp, the gravitational potential energy of a sphere at Point P, equaled mgHp, where m was the sphere's mass and g was the acceleration of the sphere due to Earth's gravity.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">2. KE<img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\197.png' width = '18'height = '42'/>, the kinetic energy of a sphere at Point L, equaled<img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\198.png' width = '88'height = '71'/>, where <img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\199.png' width = '35'height = '42'/> was the sphere's speed at Point L.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">3.<img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\200.png' width = '64'height = '42'/>, the amount of momentum of a sphere at Point L, equaled<img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\201.png' width = '60'height = '42'/>.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Then the teacher asked 3 students to predict (giving their reasons) which sphere, if either, slid farther up the left side of the incline.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Student 1</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">As a sphere slid down the incline, its GPEp was converted to kinetic energy. By the time it reached Point L, all of its GPEp had been converted. Based on Definition 1, because Sphere Y had a greater m than did Sphere X, but the same g and Hp as Sphere X, Sphere Y had a greater GPEp than did Sphere X. As a result, Sphere Y had a greater <img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\202.png' width = '54'height = '42'/> than did Sphere X. Thus, Sphere Y had a greater <img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\203.png' width = '54'height = '42'/> than did Sphere X. Thus, Sphere Y slid farther up the left side of the incline than did Sphere X.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Student 2</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Because the 2 sphere had the same a and Hp, they had the same<img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\204.png' width = '35'height = '42'/>. Based on Definition 3, because Sphere Y had a greater m than did Sphere X, Sphere Y had a greater <img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\205.png' width = '64'height = '42'/> than did Sphere X. Thus, Sphere Y slid farther up the left side of the incline than did Sphere X.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Student 3</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Because the 2 spheres had the same g and Hp, they had the same<img src='file:\$$$\resources\2011 June 69F\imgs\206.png' width = '35'height = '42'/>. Thus, they slid the same distance up the left side of the mcline.</div>
