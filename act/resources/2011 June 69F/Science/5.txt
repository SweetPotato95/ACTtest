<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">A material is superconductor (its electrical resistance, R, is zero) if its temperature, T, is lower than its critical temperature, Tc.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Students measured R and T for both a sample of Material X and a sample of Material Y while each sample was cooled. They used an ohmmeter to measure R (in ohms) and a thermocouple to measure T. The thermocouple displayed the result of each measurement of T as a voltage (in millivolts, mV). Accordingly the students plotted R versus voltage (see Figure 1). To convert voltage in mV to T in kelvins (K), they used Table 1.</div>
