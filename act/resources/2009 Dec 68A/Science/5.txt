<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">For a photon to free an electron from a metal, the photon's frequency, f, must equal or exceed the threshold frequency, <img src='file:\$$$\resources\2009 Dec 68A\imgs\204.png' width = '28'height = '36'/>, for the metal. If<img src='file:\$$$\resources\2009 Dec 68A\imgs\205.png' width = '65'height = '36'/>, the photon has extra energy that is transferred to the electron as kinetic energy, resulting in the ejection of the electron from the metal. <img src='file:\$$$\resources\2009 Dec 68A\imgs\206.png' width = '46'height = '36'/>is the highest kinetic energy an ejected electron can have for a given f.</div>
