<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Some students experimentally determined g, the acceleration due to gravity.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">In each trial, the students suspended a spring from a stand and measured the spring's length, L. They attached a mass to the suspended spring, allowed the spring-mass system to come to rest, and measured the length, y, of the extended spring (see Figure 1).</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Next, they pulled the mass down to the release point, R, and released it, allowing the mass and spring to oscillate. Using a sensor and a computer, they plotted the distance of the mass from Point R over time (see Figure 2).</div>
