
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Tomato plants grow poorly in high-salt environments. This effect is caused by 2 processes:</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">•	A net movement of H<sub>2</sub>O between the cytoplasm of the plants' cells and the environment via osmosis</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">•	An increase in the cytoplasmic Na<sup>+</sup> concentration</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">The plant Arabidopsis thaliana carries a gene, AtNHX1. The product of this gene, VAC, facilitates uptake of cytoplasmic Na<sup>+</sup> by the plant's vacuoles.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">A researcher created 4 genetically identical lines of tomato plants (L1-L4). An AtNHX1 gene from Arabidopsis thaliana was isolated and 2 identical copies of this gene were incorporated into L1's genome. This process was repeated with L2 and L3 using a different AtNHX1 allele for each line, so that L1, L2, and L3 had different genotypes for AtNHX1. The researcher then did an experiment.</div>

<br>
<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Experiment</div>

<div class = "para" style = "font-size: 16;font-family:Microsoft YAHEI;">Fifty seedlings from each of the 4 lines were grown in 10 L of nutrient solution for 80 days. The 10 L nutrient solution contained H<sub>2</sub>O, 12 g of fertilizer, and 3 g of NaCl. The nutrient solution was replaced every 5 days. After 80 days, average height, average mass (without fruit), and average fruit mass (per plant) were measured (see Table 1).</div>
